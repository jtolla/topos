DATA CLASSIFICATION AND HANDLING POLICY

Policy Name: Data Classification and Handling Policy
Policy Number: SEC-001
Policy Type: Information Security
Version: 2.1
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Next Review Date: January 1, 2025
Policy Owner: Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
Approved By: Executive Leadership Team

1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes a framework for classifying and handling data based on its sensitivity and criticality to ACME Corporation. Proper data classification ensures that information assets receive an appropriate level of protection.

2. SCOPE

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and third parties with access to ACME data
- All data created, collected, stored, or processed by ACME Corporation
- All systems and applications that handle ACME data
- All locations where ACME data is stored or processed

3. DATA CLASSIFICATION LEVELS

3.1 PUBLIC
- Information approved for public release
- No restrictions on distribution
- Examples: Marketing materials, press releases, public website content

3.2 INTERNAL
- Information intended for internal use only
- Should not be shared externally without authorization
- Examples: Internal announcements, organizational charts, general policies

3.3 CONFIDENTIAL
- Sensitive business information requiring protection
- Disclosure could harm business interests
- Examples: Financial reports, business strategies, customer lists, contracts

3.4 RESTRICTED
- Highly sensitive information requiring maximum protection
- Disclosure could cause significant harm
- Examples:
  * Personal Identifiable Information (PII): SSN, credit card numbers
  * Protected Health Information (PHI)
  * Trade secrets and intellectual property
  * Security credentials and API keys
  * Employee salary and performance data

4. DATA HANDLING REQUIREMENTS

4.1 PUBLIC DATA
- Storage: No special requirements
- Transmission: No encryption required
- Disposal: Standard disposal methods

4.2 INTERNAL DATA
- Storage: Access controls required
- Transmission: Internal networks only; encryption for external transmission
- Disposal: Delete or shred when no longer needed

4.3 CONFIDENTIAL DATA
- Storage: Encrypted at rest; access logging required
- Transmission: Encryption required (TLS 1.2+)
- Disposal: Secure deletion with verification
- Retention: Per retention schedule, minimum 7 years for financial data

4.4 RESTRICTED DATA
- Storage: Encrypted at rest (AES-256); strict access controls; dedicated systems
- Transmission: End-to-end encryption required
- Disposal: Cryptographic erasure or physical destruction
- Access: Need-to-know basis; manager approval required
- Monitoring: Real-time access monitoring and alerts

5. LABELING REQUIREMENTS

5.1 All documents containing Confidential or Restricted data must be clearly labeled.

5.2 Electronic files should include classification in:
- File name (e.g., "Q4-Financial-Report-CONFIDENTIAL.xlsx")
- Document header/footer
- Metadata where supported

5.3 Physical documents must have classification stamps on each page.

6. ACCESS CONTROL

6.1 Access to classified data must follow the principle of least privilege.

6.2 Access requests must be:
- Submitted through the IT Service Portal
- Approved by data owner
- Reviewed quarterly

6.3 Access must be revoked:
- Upon role change
- Upon termination
- When no longer required

7. THIRD-PARTY SHARING

7.1 Confidential and Restricted data may only be shared with third parties under:
- Signed NDA or equivalent contractual protection
- Data owner approval
- Legal review for Restricted data

7.2 Third parties must demonstrate adequate security controls.

8. INCIDENT REPORTING

8.1 Any suspected data breach or policy violation must be reported immediately to:
- Security Operations Center: security@acme-corp.example.com
- Security Hotline: (555) 911-SECU

8.2 Do not attempt to investigate or contain incidents independently.

9. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

9.1 Compliance with this policy is mandatory.

9.2 Violations may result in:
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Civil or criminal penalties for willful violations
- Liability for damages resulting from unauthorized disclosure

9.3 Regular audits will be conducted to verify compliance.

10. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

10.1 All employees must complete data classification training:
- Within 30 days of hire
- Annually thereafter

10.2 Additional training required for personnel handling Restricted data.

11. EXCEPTIONS

11.1 Exceptions to this policy must be:
- Submitted in writing to the CISO
- Approved by the CISO and data owner
- Time-limited and documented
- Reviewed quarterly
