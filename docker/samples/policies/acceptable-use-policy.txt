ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY

Policy Name: Acceptable Use Policy
Policy Number: SEC-002
Policy Type: Information Security
Version: 3.0
Effective Date: March 1, 2024
Next Review Date: March 1, 2025
Policy Owner: Chief Information Officer (CIO)
Approved By: Executive Leadership Team

1. PURPOSE

This Acceptable Use Policy ("Policy") defines the acceptable use of information technology resources at ACME Corporation. This policy protects employees, partners, and the company from illegal or damaging actions.

2. SCOPE

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary workers
- All equipment owned or leased by ACME Corporation
- All network resources and internet access provided by ACME
- Personal devices used for company business (BYOD)

3. GENERAL USE AND OWNERSHIP

3.1 Company Resources
- IT resources are provided for business purposes
- Limited personal use is permitted if it does not interfere with work
- Users have no expectation of privacy when using company resources
- All data created on company systems is company property

3.2 User Responsibilities
- Users are responsible for the security of their accounts
- Passwords must not be shared or written down
- Workstations must be locked when unattended
- Suspicious activity must be reported immediately

4. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

The following activities are strictly prohibited:

4.1 Security Violations
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Circumventing security controls
- Installing unauthorized software
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the network
- Sharing credentials or access tokens
- Port scanning or network reconnaissance without authorization

4.2 Illegal Activities
- Violating copyright, trade secret, or patent laws
- Unauthorized copying of licensed software
- Exporting controlled data in violation of export laws
- Any activity that violates local, state, or federal law

4.3 Communication Violations
- Sending harassing, threatening, or offensive messages
- Distributing spam or chain letters
- Impersonating others
- Unauthorized mass mailings

4.4 Data Handling Violations
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
- Storing sensitive data on unapproved systems
- Transmitting unencrypted sensitive data
- Uploading company data to unauthorized cloud services

4.5 System Misuse
- Using excessive bandwidth for non-business purposes
- Running cryptocurrency mining software
- Using company resources for personal business ventures
- Intentionally introducing malware

5. EMAIL AND COMMUNICATION

5.1 Email Usage
- Business email should be used for business purposes
- Sensitive information must be encrypted
- Auto-forwarding to external addresses is prohibited
- Users must verify recipient addresses before sending sensitive data

5.2 Messaging and Collaboration
- Approved platforms: Slack, Microsoft Teams, Zoom
- Confidential discussions should use private channels
- Meeting recordings require participant consent

6. INTERNET AND WEB USAGE

6.1 Acceptable Use
- Research related to job functions
- Professional development
- Limited personal browsing during breaks

6.2 Prohibited Sites
- Adult or explicit content
- Gambling sites
- Illegal download or streaming sites
- Hacking or exploit sites
- Sites promoting violence or hate

6.3 Downloads
- Only download files necessary for business
- Verify source legitimacy before downloading
- Report suspicious downloads to IT Security

7. MOBILE AND REMOTE ACCESS

7.1 Mobile Devices
- Company mobile devices must have MDM software installed
- Personal devices used for work must meet security requirements
- Lost or stolen devices must be reported within 1 hour

7.2 Remote Work
- Use VPN for all remote access to company resources
- Work only from secure, private locations
- Do not use public Wi-Fi without VPN
- Ensure home networks are secured with WPA3

8. SOCIAL MEDIA

8.1 Personal Use
- Do not disclose confidential company information
- Do not speak on behalf of the company without authorization
- Clearly state opinions are personal, not company views

8.2 Official Accounts
- Only authorized personnel may manage company accounts
- All posts must be approved by Marketing/Communications
- Customer complaints must be escalated, not addressed directly

9. MONITORING AND PRIVACY

9.1 Company Rights
- ACME reserves the right to monitor all IT resource usage
- This includes email, web browsing, file access, and network traffic
- Monitoring may occur without notice

9.2 User Acknowledgment
- By using company resources, users consent to monitoring
- Users should have no expectation of privacy on company systems

10. INCIDENT REPORTING

10.1 Users must report:
- Security incidents or suspicious activity
- Lost or stolen devices
- Suspected policy violations
- Phishing attempts

10.2 Report to:
- IT Help Desk: helpdesk@acme-corp.example.com
- Security Team: security@acme-corp.example.com
- Anonymous Hotline: (555) 123-TIPS

11. ENFORCEMENT

11.1 Violations of this policy may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Suspension of access privileges
- Termination of employment
- Civil or criminal prosecution

11.2 Severity of consequences depends on:
- Nature and severity of the violation
- Whether the violation was intentional
- Prior violations
- Impact on the company

12. EXCEPTIONS

Exceptions must be:
- Requested in writing
- Approved by IT Security and department manager
- Documented and time-limited
- Reviewed upon expiration

13. TRAINING

All users must complete:
- Security awareness training upon hire
- Annual refresher training
- Additional training as required by role

14. POLICY REVIEW

This policy will be reviewed annually and updated as needed.
